Conversation Signals

The words and phrases the AI listens for during every call — shared across all companies

What is this? Every time a caller speaks, the AI checks their words against these lists to understand what they mean. For example: when offering an upsell, the AI needs to know if the caller said "yes" or "no". When the caller is ready to book, the AI recognises phrases like "schedule a visit".

Why here? "Yes" is "yes" whether you're a plumber or a dentist — these signals are the same for every company on the platform. Managing them in one place prevents drift and keeps every company's AI consistent. Changes go live on the next call (no restart needed).

Phrase Intelligence

Reduces caller phrases to routing-optimised cores — powers Tier 3 scoring in KC phrase quality

What is this? When a caller says "how much is a service call?", the AI strips the noise ("how much is a" → cost) and keeps the routing core (cost service call). The core is then embedded and compared to section content — this is Tier 3 scoring.

Why global? "How much" means "cost" in every industry. Intent normalizers, stop words, synonym groups, and danger words are universal English — shared across all companies. Protected domain phrases (like "service call" or "maintenance plan") are auto-extracted per-company from KC content. Changes go live on the next phrase score (no restart needed).

LAP Keyword Groups

System-level attention keywords — all companies inherit these. Each company adds custom keywords on top via their UAP settings.

What is this? LAP (ListenerActParser) fires on every call turn, every state — even during booking field collection. When a caller says "hello?" (bad connection), "hold on" (needs a moment), or "what?" (didn't catch that), LAP intercepts before the booking engine sees it. No more "Mark Hell" from accepting "Hello" as a last name.

Why global? "Hold on" means the same thing for every caller regardless of which company they're calling. System keywords are admin-managed here and inherited by all companies. Companies extend them with their own words in their UAP settings. Changes go live on the next call (no restart needed).
